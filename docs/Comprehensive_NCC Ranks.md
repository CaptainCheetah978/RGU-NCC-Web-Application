Here is the comprehensive breakdown of the Official Leadership (Regular Army and Institutional staff) that oversees the cadets. These are the authorities who give the orders that the CSUOs and other cadet ranks execute.



\## Battalion Level (Regular Army Officers)

These are the professional military officers who command the entire NCC unit.

1. **Commanding Officer** (CO): A Colonel/Lt. Colonel (Army), Captain/Commander (Navy), or Group Captain/Wing Commander (Air Force). He is the ultimate authority for the unit, overseeing all training, discipline, and administration.
2. **Administrative Officer** (Adm Offr): A Major/Lt. Colonel (Army), Lt. Commander/Commander (Navy), or Squadron Leader/Wing Commander (Air Force). He manages the unit’s budget, planning, and logistics for all camps.
3. **Senior Instructional Staff**: A Subedar Major (Army), Master Chief Petty Officer (Navy), or Master Warrant Officer (Air Force). They are the most senior non-commissioned personnel and advise the CO on drill and discipline.
4. **Permanent Instructional Staff** (PI Staff): Regular soldiers/sailors/airmen (JCOs, NCOs, Petty Officers, or Sergeants) who provide the actual hands-on training to cadets in drill and weaponry.





\## Institutional Level (College or School Staff)



&#x20;  1. **Head of Institution** (HOI): The Principal (for school or college), Vice-Chancellor, or Registrar (for university). While they don't wear a uniform, they are legally responsible for the NCC unit within their campus. They provide the infrastructure (NCC room, parade ground) and ensure that the NCC activities align with the academic calendar.

&#x20;  2. **Associate NCC Officer** (ANO): A member of the college/university faculty who has undergone official training at the Officers Training Academy (OTA Kamptee or Gwalior). They hold an NCC Commission with ranks like Lieutenant, Captain, or Major (Army)/Sub Lieutenant, Lieutenant, Lt. Commander (Navy)/Flying Officer, Flight Lieutenant,Squadron Leader (Air Force). They are your immediate commanding officer in the college and the person the CSUO reports to directly.

&#x20;  3. **Caretaker Officer** (CTO): A lecturer or teacher of the instition who looks after the NCC unit when there is no trained ANO available. They perform the administrative duties of an ANO but have not yet completed the formal military training or received a commission rank.





Now for cadets' rank:



1. **Cadet Senior Under Officer** (CSUO) **/** **Senior Cadet Captain** (Navy)**/ Senior Under Officer** (Air): The highest cadet rank. In a Battalion, they act as the Assistant Adjutant (administration) or Assistant Quartermaster (unit stores). In a Company/Division, they act as the Assistant Company Commander, serving as the senior-most student leader and the primary link between the ANO and the cadets.
2. **Cadet Junior Under Officer** (CJUO/CUO) **/** **Cadet Captain** (Navy) **/ Cadet Junior Under Officer** (Air)**:** The second-highest rank. In a unit, they act as the Platoon Commander (Army/Air) or Divisional Commander (Navy), leading 30–40 cadets and assisting the CSUO in daily operations.
3. **Company Sergeant Major** (CSM): A senior NCO appointment at the college level—usually a CSM (Army), PO Cadet (Navy), or CWO (Air Force). They act as the "Enforcer," managing the "Muster Roll" (attendance), uniform turnout, and leading the company on the parade ground.
4. **Company Quartermaster Sergeant** (CQMS): A senior NCO appointment at the college level—usually a CQMS (Army), PO Cadet (Navy), or CWO (Air Force). They act as the Logistics Manager for the college, handling the local armory (Kote) and refreshments.
5. **Sergeant** (SGT) **/** **Petty Officer Cadet** (Navy) **/ Sergeant** (Air): The Section Commander. They lead a small group of 10–12 cadets and are the primary instructors for basic drill and ground-level discipline.
6. **Corporal** (CPL) **/** **Leading Cadet** (Navy) **/** **Corporal** (Air): The Section 2IC (Second-in-Command). They assist the Sergeant and often handle specialized roles like the Provost Sergeant (Police) or Water/Sanitation Duty during camps.
7. **Lance Corporal** (L/CPL) **/** **Cadet Class I** (Navy)  **Leading Flight Cadet** (Air): The Entry-level Leader. They assist the Corporals and are put in charge of very small groups (3–5 cadets) for minor tasks like fatigue duty or guiding first-year cadets.
8. **Cadet /** **Cadet Class II** (Navy)  **Flight Cadet** (Air) : The Foundation/Trainee. Their role is to learn the syllabus, follow all orders from the NCOs and Under Officers, and maintain the high standards of the NCC.
